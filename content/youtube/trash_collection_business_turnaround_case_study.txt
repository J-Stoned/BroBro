BUSINESS METRICS:

Revenue: $642,000
Net Profit: -$151,000
Net Margins: -23%
Time in Business: Under 2 years
Current Customers: 2,500 (1,000 scatter + 13 HOAs representing 1,500 doors)

Customer Types:
1. Scatter Customers (50% of revenue = $300K)
   - Once weekly trash, recycling, and yard waste collection
   - No existing contracts - can start service immediately
   - Pricing: $29.67/month (billed quarterly at $89)
   - Intro offer: 3 months free (on invoices 2, 3, and 4)
   - CAC: $51
   - LTV: $1,000
   - LTV:CAC ratio: 21:1
   - Gross Margin: 26-40% (currently 26%, potential 40% with efficiency)
   - Monthly churn: 0.4%

2. HOA Customers (50% of revenue = $300K)
   - Once or twice weekly trash collection (80% choose twice weekly)
   - 3-5 year contracts
   - Pricing varies: depends on home type, unit count, proximity to routes
   - Monthly billing schedule
   - CAC: $1,200
   - LTV: $23,000
   - LTV:CAC ratio: 18:1
   - Gross Margin: 23%
   - Only 13 HOA customers total
   - 9 more HOAs in pipeline

Market Context:
- Service area: Few million people in 10-20 mile radius
- Market composition: ~70% HOAs, ~30% scatter homes
- One major competitor aggressively targeting scatter customers
- Recently acquired competitor that only did HOAs (customers unhappy)

ACQUISITION CHANNELS:

1. Door-to-Door (50% of customers)
   - Philip: 300 doors/week, 26% close rate, ~30% door open rate
   - Closes 1 sale per 12 doors knocked
   - Recently hired sales rep: $50 commission per sale
   - Sales rep performance: Started 3 weeks ago, averaging 5/day, hit 7 yesterday
   - No upfront costs besides commission

2. Paid Ads ($600/month)
   - Meta ads targeting scatter customers
   - Total ad spend: $7,500 historical
   - Cost per click: $2.12
   - Total clicks: 3,500

   - LTV:CAC: 20:1
   - Challenge: Cost per lead increases dramatically when scaling spend
   - Targeting small geographic area

3. Cold Outreach
   - Email (list exhausted quickly)
   - Handwritten letters to HOA decision makers
   - Total spend: $12,000
   - Results: 9 HOAs in pipeline
   - ROI: 700:1 on closed deals

   - Scatter customers get 1 free month per successful referral

   - Needs better nurturing

Organic Lead Generation:
- Average 217 leads/month total
- Close 72 (33% close rate)

- HOAs: 2 leads/month average, 50% close rate
- Bins provided: Trash + recycling containers (gray color)

COST STRUCTURE:

Per Customer Economics:
- Cost per pickup per month: $14 (labor + truck + dump fees)
- For quarterly billing: $14 x 3 = $42 in costs before next payment
- Bin cost: $116 (currently financed, used to pay cash)
- Setup/onboarding: ~$2.50 per customer if outsourced ($500 for 200 customers)
- Sales commission: $50 per scatter customer

Fully Loaded First Quarter Cost:
- $42 (3 months of service)
- $50 (commission)
- $116 (bin - amortized)
- Total: $208 to break even on first transaction

Current Cash Collection (Old Model):
- Scatter: $89 first quarter (paying $50 commission + $42 service = -$3 before bin)
- Not collecting enough upfront to cover costs
- Creating cash flow crisis

STRATEGIC DECISIONS MADE:

Decision #1: Avatar Selection - SCATTER
Reasoning:
- HOAs are commoditized auction markets (race to the bottom on price)
- Scatter has higher gross margins (26-40% vs 23%)
- Scatter allows premium pricing and outmarketing competition
- Door-to-door is highly scalable and proven (1 sale per 12 doors)
- Scatter customers do once-weekly (vs twice) = higher truck throughput
- Can still accept inbound HOA deals but not primary growth strategy

Decision #2: Acquisition Channel - DOOR-TO-DOOR
Reasoning:
- Already 50% of customer acquisition
- Proven 26% close rate with 30% door open rate
- New rep ramping to 7 sales/day after just 3 weeks
- Commission-only model = no risk
- Naturally creates efficient routes
- Target: 10 sales reps x 5 sales/day = 50 sales/day = 1,500/month = 18,000/year
- Would reach 20,000 customers (far exceeding 3,000 profitability threshold)
- No limit on houses available for door-knocking

THE OFFER REDESIGN:

Key Constraints to Solve:
1. Need $208 upfront to break even (commission + 3 months service + bin)
2. Current offer only collecting $89 first quarter
3. Competitor offering 3 months free at similar pricing
4. Need to maintain competitive advantage while improving cash flow

Offer Levers Available:
1. QR code on bins (ugly but free advertising vs. premium clean look)
2. Bin ownership/financing
3. Commitment length (quarterly vs annual)
4. Initiation/setup fees
5. Price per month

FINAL OFFER STRUCTURE:

Option A - Annual Commitment (Anchor/Premium):
- $30/month x 12 months = $360 paid upfront
- FREE $99 bin (company absorbs cost)
- Still includes 3 months free (built into pricing)
- Bin becomes customer property
- No initiation fee

Option B - Quarterly Commitment (Core Offer):
- $30/month billed quarterly
- $89 first quarter payment
- PLUS $99 bin purchase (customer owns it)
- Total first transaction: $188
- Still includes 3 months free on quarters 2, 3, 4 ($59.67 per quarter after)
- Bin becomes customer property
- If customer cancels early, company takes bin back

Bin Financing Alternative:
- If customer doesn't want to pay $99 upfront for bin
- Finance $150 over 3-year term
- Locks customer into 3-year contract
- Build in ~4% annual price increase

Blended Average Cash Per Door Math:
- Assume 80% take Option B ($189)
- Assume 20% take Option A ($360)
- 80% x $189 = $151.20
- 20% x $360 = $72
- Blended average: $223.20 per door
- Target needed: $208
- EXCEEDS TARGET by $15.20

Why This Works:
1. Solves cash flow problem immediately
2. Maintains competitive 3 months free offer
3. Bin purchase feels fair (they own it)
4. Annual option provides massive anchor ($360 vs $189 makes $189 feel reasonable)
5. Sales reps only need to explain two simple options
6. Commission still covers itself from first payment
7. Even if only 80% of customers take core offer, still profitable

Sales Script Framework:
"We're giving you 3 months free either way because you're switching to us. If you're willing to commit to the whole year, I'll give you the bin - it's on me. If you'd rather just try us out for the first quarter, no worries, we'll get you set up, you just need to purchase the bin for $99 and it's yours to keep."

Key Psychological Elements:
- 3 months free = parity with competitor (removes objection)
- Bin ownership = tangible asset (not just paying fees)
- Annual option makes quarterly seem like a deal
- "It's yours to keep" = ownership mentality reduces churn
- Simple A/B choice = easy decision making

Additional Revenue Optimizations:
- QR code on bins: $5/month upcharge to remove it (most will keep it)
- Processing fee: 3% on credit cards (waived if they provide 2 payment methods)
- Creates backup payment method and reduces churn from card declines

BUSINESS MODEL INSIGHTS:

Why Trash Collection is Hard:
- Capital intensive (trucks cost $100K+ in repairs alone)
- Commoditized service (hard to differentiate)
- Route efficiency critical to profitability
- Upfront costs before revenue (bins, onboarding, first service)
- Competitor pressure on pricing

Why This Can Work:
- Extremely low churn (0.4% monthly)
- High LTV:CAC ratios (18-21:1)
- Proven door-to-door acquisition (26% close rate)
- Organic growth from branded bins
- Switching costs for customers (inertia)
- Essential service (not discretionary)

The Key Insight:
"If only one thing were true - that you could get 10 commission-only door-to-door reps doing 5 sales per day - then what stops you from hitting your goal?"

Answer: Cash flow from not collecting enough upfront. Solution: Redesign offer to collect $223 per door instead of $89.

LESSONS FOR SERVICE BUSINESSES:

1. Avatar Clarity Matters:
   - Don't try to serve two different avatars equally
   - Choose based on: margins, acquisition cost, scalability, commoditization risk
   - Can serve both but PRIMARY focus on one

2. Acquisition Channel Focus:
   - Better to master one channel than be mediocre at five
   - Door-to-door works when: high door-open rates, clear value prop, commission model
   - Commission-only removes risk and allows rapid scaling

3. Offer Design is Critical:
   - Must collect enough upfront to cover: CAC + initial service costs
   - Anchoring works (high annual price makes quarterly seem reasonable)
   - Giving customers ownership (bin) reduces perceived "fees"
   - Simplicity in presentation (A or B choice)

4. Cash Flow vs. Profitability:
   - Can be profitable on paper but cash-flow negative
   - Upfront costs (commission, bins, service) must be covered by first payment
   - Financing customer purchases (bins) creates 3-year lock-in

5. Scaling with Constraints:
   - When one variable can solve everything, focus there ruthlessly
   - "If only this were true, what stops me?" - removes distractions
   - Capital-intensive businesses need cash-flow solutions first

6. Competition Response:
   - Don't race to the bottom on price
   - Find different value (bin ownership, 3 months free, annual savings)
   - Compete on different dimension than main competitor

STRATEGIC PRINCIPLES DEMONSTRATED:

1. Simplicity over Complexity:
   - Two-offer structure vs. complicated tier system
   - One avatar vs. trying to serve everyone
   - One primary channel vs. spreading thin

2. Economics Drive Strategy:
   - If economics don't work, strategy doesn't matter
   - Must collect enough upfront to cover costs
   - Blended average allows for offer variation

3. Leverage Commission Model:
   - Zero risk scaling (only pay on results)
   - Can hire 10 reps without cash outlay
   - Reps improve over time (3 weeks to 7 sales/day)

4. Anchoring and Framing:
   - $360 annual makes $189 quarterly seem cheap
   - "Own the bin" feels better than "pay setup fee"
   - "3 months free" matches competitor but collects more upfront

5. Constraints Breed Creativity:
   - "How do we collect $208?" forced offer innovation
   - Limited by cash flow, not opportunity
   - Bin financing creates 3-year contracts

APPLICABLE TO OTHER BUSINESSES:

This case study applies to any business with:
- High upfront costs (CAC + onboarding + initial service)
- Subscription/recurring revenue model
- Multiple potential avatars (need to choose)
- Multiple potential channels (need to focus)
- Capital constraints
- Local service delivery

The framework:
1. Calculate true first-transaction breakeven (CAC + initial costs)
2. Design offer to collect that amount upfront (or more)
3. Choose ONE avatar and ONE channel that can scale
4. Focus ruthlessly until goal achieved
5. Use anchoring and offer structure to improve conversion AND cash collected

This is a masterclass in business turnaround through offer design, avatar clarity, and channel focus.
