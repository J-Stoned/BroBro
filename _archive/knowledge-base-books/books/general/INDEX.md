**Author:** Robert B. Cialdini, Ph.D. - Social psychologist, expert on persuasion and compliance research. Spent 3 years in participant observation studying "compliance professionals" (sales operators, fund-raisers, recruiters, advertisers).

**Core Concepts - The 6 Weapons of Influence:**

1. **Reciprocation** (Chapter 2)
   - "The Old Give and Take...and Take"
   - People feel obligated to return favors
   - Free samples, gifts, concessions trigger repayment

2. **Commitment and Consistency** (Chapter 3)
   - "Hobgoblins of the Mind"
   - Once committed, people align future behavior with past actions
   - Small commitments lead to larger ones (foot-in-the-door)

3. **Social Proof** (Chapter 4)
   - "Truths Are Us"
   - People look to others to determine correct behavior
   - Testimonials, reviews, "best-seller" labels

4. **Liking** (Chapter 5)
   - "The Friendly Thief"
   - People comply with those they like
   - Similarity, compliments, familiarity, association

5. **Authority** (Chapter 6)
   - "Directed Deference"
   - People follow perceived experts/authorities
   - Titles, uniforms, credentials, expertise signals

6. **Scarcity** (Chapter 7)
   - "The Rule of the Few"
   - Limited availability increases perceived value
   - Deadlines, limited quantities, exclusive access
