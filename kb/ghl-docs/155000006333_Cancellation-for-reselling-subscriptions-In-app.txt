What is In-App Cancellation for Reselling Subscriptions?
This capability enables users working inside a
subaccount
to view the add-ons they’re subscribed to (e.g., WhatsApp, Listings, WordPress, AI tools) and cancel those
resold Marketplace subscriptions
from the same Billing area where they manage payments and wallet transactions. The experience mirrors agency-side controls but is purpose-built for subaccount access and day-to-day management.
Key Benefits of In-App Cancellation for Reselling Subscriptions
Understanding the advantages helps teams decide when to use subaccount-level cancellation versus contacting the agency. The items below focus on clarity, control, and a consistent experience across add-ons.
Self-service control:
subaccount users can cancel eligible add-on subscriptions without agency intervention.
Visibility of details:
see
Plan, Purchase Type, Status, Purchase Date, and Billing Cycle
before taking action.
Unified cancellation flow:
a standardized, multi-step unsubscribe modal across products keeps the process predictable.
Timing options:
choose
End of billing cycle
(default) or
Cancel immediately
as appropriate.
Reactivation window:
if you select
end-of-cycle
, you can reactivate before the effective date.
Operational efficiency:
fewer back-and-forth requests between subaccounts and agencies.

View Subscription Details
Reviewing subscription details first helps you pick the right timing option and verify you’re canceling the intended plan, especially when multiple add-ons are active. Navigate to Subaccount → Settings → Billing → Subscriptions. In the
Add On Products
panel, locate the product card and click
View Subscriptions
.
What you’ll see (modal columns):
Plan:
the subscribed plan name for the product.
Purchase Type:
where/how it was purchased (e.g., “Agency Purchase (Without Upsell)”).
Status:
e.g., Active, Active (Trial).
Purchase Date:
the date the subscription began.
Billing Cycle:
the cadence for renewals.
Actions (⋮):
management menu, including
Cancel Subscription
.
How To Set Up In-App Cancellation for Reselling Subscriptions
No separate configuration is required at the subaccount level—the feature runs where you already manage billing. Use the steps below any time you need to review or cancel a resold add-on.
Step 1:
Go to Subaccount Settings → Billing → Subscriptions.
Step 2:
On the relevant product card, click View Subscriptions.
Step 3:
In the modal, review Plan, Purchase Type, Status, Purchase Date, Billing Cycle. Once reviewed the correct subscription to cancel,
Click Actions (⋮) → Cancel Subscription.
Step 4:
In the unified modal, choose End of billing cycle (default) or Cancel immediately, then Proceed to cancellation. Complete the remaining confirmation steps until you see success feedback.
Frequently Asked Questions
Q. Who can cancel resold add-on subscriptions now?
A. Both
agency users
(from Agency Billing) and
subaccount users
(from Subaccount Billing) can cancel supported add-ons. This article focuses on the
subaccount
flow.
Q. Will I be charged again after I cancel?
A. If you select
End of billing cycle
, billing stops on the
effective date
shown in the modal. If you
cancel immediately
, the subscription stops right away, and
unused period of service will not be provided
.
Q. Can I undo a cancellation?
A. If you chose
End of billing cycle
, you can
reactivate before the effective date
from the same area where you initiated cancellation (if the option is available for that add-on).
Q. Why don’t I see the Cancel option?
A. Confirm you are in the
correct subaccount
, have
Billing
access, and are looking at an
eligible add-on
. Some add-ons or billing sources may not support in-app cancellation.
Q. What’s the difference between SaaS plan cancellation and add-on cancellation?
A.
SaaS plan
covers the core subaccount subscription and is managed via SaaS tools;
add-ons
are resold Marketplace products managed from
Subaccount Billing → Subscriptions
.
Q. Do product-specific rules still apply?
A. The
unified unsubscribe modal
governs timing and confirmation. Some products may have extra considerations (e.g., usage-based charges that settle after cancel).
